# For-Arrangører Page: Complete Copy Rewrite

**Voice:** A Bergen product talking to Bergen venue owners. Professional but warm.
**Pronoun:** "Gåri" as the product/subject, "vi" (we) for actions. Never "jeg" (I) — the page represents the product, not one person.
**Test:** If it sounds like a meeting room with a projector, rewrite it. If it sounds like a café with a laptop open, it's right.

## Page narrative arc

The page tells a story in 5 beats:

1. **Hva er dette?** — Gåri is Bergen's digital town square. Everything in one place. (Hero)
2. **Hvordan fungerer dette?** — Gåri visits venues twice daily, picks up new events automatically. You do nothing. (Flow animation)
3. **Hvorfor dette fungerer?** — Network effect: breadth creates habit, free pulls in paid. Plus: 54% of Norwegians use AI tools, and Gåri optimizes for that. (Network effect + AI pitch)
4. **Hva får jeg?** — Curated pages, AI visibility, newsletter, monthly report. (Feature cards with mockups)
5. **Hva nå?** — 3 months free. No tricks. Get in touch. (CTA)

---

## Copy rules

1. **"Gåri" as product subject, "vi" for actions.** "Gåri samler alt som skjer" and "Vi sender publikum videre til deg." Never "jeg" or "meg". Never "vår plattform."
2. **Start sentences with the venue's thing, not Gåri's thing.** "Arrangementene dine dukker opp" not "Gåri gjør arrangementene dine synlige."
3. **No word appears that a venue owner wouldn't use at lunch.** If they wouldn't say "fremhevet synlighet" in conversation, neither should we.
4. **Every paragraph under 35 words.** If it's longer, it has two ideas — split it.
5. **Headings under 6 words.** "3 måneder gratis" (3). "Hvorfor dette fungerer" (3).
6. **Concrete numbers beat vague claims.** "13 sider" not "flere kuraterte sider." "Kl. 06 og 18" not "to ganger daglig."
7. **The English isn't translated — it's rewritten.** Each version sounds natural in its own language.
8. **Honest AI claims.** "jobber vi for" / "optimaliserer for" — never guarantee AI citation.

```
Bergens Digitale Bytorg

Gåri er et digitalt bytorg for Bergen.

[Ta kontakt]
```

#### English
```
Bergen's Digital Town Square

Gåri is a digital town square for Bergen.
Everything happening, gathered in one place.

[Get in touch]
```

**Why this works:**
- "Bergens Digitale Bytorg" — names the concept immediately. A town square, digital, for Bergen.
- Body is 2 sentences. Defines what Gåri is and what it does. No elaboration needed.
- The network effect argument (WHY a town square works) lives in section 3, not here.

## SECTION 2: How it works (Hvordan fungerer dette)

**Strategy:** Show the mechanism. One thing: Gåri collects automatically. Animation does the heavy lifting.

#### Norwegian
```
Gåri henter arrangementene — automatisk

To ganger om dagen besøker Gåri nettsidene til steder
som arrangerer ting i Bergen. Når noe nytt dukker opp —
en konsert, en utstilling, en quiz-kveld — tar Gåri det
med tilbake og viser det frem på bytorget.

Du trenger ikke gjøre noe.
```

#### English
```
Gåri fetches events — automatically

Twice a day, Gåri visits the websites of venues organizing
things in Bergen. When something new appears — a concert,
an exhibition, a quiz night — Gåri brings it back and
displays it on the town square.

You don't need to do anything.
```

**Why this works:**
- "Gåri henter arrangementene" — product as subject, active verb.
- "Du trenger ikke gjøre noe" — strongest reassurance. Standalone emphasis line.
- The flow animation (venue pills → Gåri badge) shows the mechanism visually.

## SECTION 3a: Network effect (Hvorfor dette fungerer)

**Strategy:** Three paragraphs, each one idea. The bytorg metaphor → the mechanism → why it matters to YOU.

#### Norwegian
```
Hvorfor dette fungerer

Et bytorg med tre boder er ikke et bytorg. Det er først
når bredden er der — konserter, teater, matfestivaler,
quizkvelder, gratisarrangementer — at folk begynner å
sjekke innom som vane.

Gratis arrangementer trekker folk inn. Betalte arrangementer
tjener på trafikken. Studenten som finner en gratis quizkveld
i dag, kjøper konsertbillett neste uke. Uten det første
besøket hadde det andre aldri skjedd.

Jo mer som er samlet på torget, jo flere grunner har folk
til å komme tilbake. Jo oftere de kommer tilbake, jo mer
ser de av dine arrangementer.
```

#### English
```
Why this works

A town square with three stalls isn't a town square. It's
only when the breadth is there — concerts, theatre, food
festivals, quiz nights, free events — that people start
checking in as a habit.

Free events draw people in. Paid events benefit from the
traffic. The student who finds a free quiz night today buys
a concert ticket next week. Without the first visit, the
second would never have happened.

The more that's gathered on the square, the more reasons
people have to come back. The more often they come back,
the more they see of your events.
```

**Why this works:**
- P1: Defines the problem (incomplete square doesn't work) and when it DOES work (breadth + habit)
- P2: The mechanism (free → paid pipeline, the quiz→concert chain)
- P3: Directly addresses the venue owner ("dine arrangementer")
- No analogy explaining itself. No "Samme mekanisme som..." — trust the reader.
