# For-Arrangører Page: Complete Copy Rewrite

**Voice:** A Bergen product talking to Bergen venue owners. Professional but warm.
**Pronoun:** "Gåri" as the product/subject, "vi" (we) for actions. Never "jeg" (I) — the page represents the product, not one person.
**Test:** If it sounds like a meeting room with a projector, rewrite it. If it sounds like a café with a laptop open, it's right.

> **UPDATE 2026-02-26:** Voice changed from first-person "jeg" to product voice "Gåri/vi". All copy examples below updated to reflect this.

---

## What's wrong with the current copy

Before the rewrite, here's what I'd flag if I were editing someone else's draft:

**1. The hero says two things at once.** "Synlig der folk faktisk søker" is abstract — it could be about Google, AI, social media, anything. Then the subtext tries to explain all three in one breath. A hero needs ONE clear thought.

**2. The hero and section 2 are the same section.** Both say "Gåri er en av kildene som blir sitert." The reader hits the same point twice and feels like they're re-reading.

**3. "Fremhevet synlighet" is product jargon.** No venue owner in Bergen wakes up thinking "I need fremhevet synlighet." They think "I need more people to find out about our event." Write for how they think, not how you categorize.

**4. The bytorget section is an essay.** Three paragraphs to make one point. The punchline — "Det er ikke veldedighet. Det er god forretning for alle." — is buried under explanation. Lead with the insight, not the setup.

**5. "Vi hjelper arrangører å nå folk" — who talks like this?** This is agency copy. Better: "Gåri sender folk til deg" or "Dine arrangementer dukker opp når noen spør ChatGPT."

**6. Every section starts with "Vi."** Vi samler. Vi hjelper. Vi sender. Vi leter. It reads like a company deck, not a conversation. Mix it up — start with what the venue gets, not what Gåri does. Use "Gåri" as subject where possible.

**7. "Transparent alltid" is a legal disclaimer.** It should be confidence: "Ingen skjulte triks."

**8. The early bird buries the offer.** "Vi leter etter de første arrangørene som vil teste..." — 15 words before you get to the point. The point is "3 måneder gratis."

**9. The CTA is corporate.** "Ta kontakt" means nothing. Everyone's CTA is "Ta kontakt." Make it direct and specific: "Ta kontakt" is acceptable if simple, or use "Vis meg dataen for mine arrangementer" for specificity.

## The Rewrite

### SECTION 1: Hero

**Strategy:** One thought. Hook the reader. Under 15 words for the subtext.

#### Norwegian
```
Arrangementene dine i ChatGPT-svar

Gåri samler alt som skjer i Bergen — og gjør det
synlig i AI-søk, på Google og i ukentlige nyhetsbrev.

[Ta kontakt]
```

#### English
```
Your events in ChatGPT answers

Gåri brings together everything happening in Bergen — and makes it
visible in AI search, on Google and in weekly newsletters.

[Get in touch]
```

**Why this works:**
- "Arrangementene dine i ChatGPT-svar" — starts with THEIR events, not Gåri. Specific enough to intrigue ("ChatGPT-svar?"), short enough to scan.
- Subtext is 19 words. States the three channels clearly: AI search, Google, newsletter. Done.
- "Ta kontakt" — simple, direct, no friction.

**What I cut:**
- "Synlig der folk faktisk søker" — too vague, could mean anything
- "Vi hjelper arrangører å nå folk som søker med AI" — restates the headline
- The entire second sentence of the old subtext — it just repeated the first

### SECTION 2: AI Search (the differentiator)

**Strategy:** Lead with a hard number that stops scrolling. Then explain what it means for them.

#### Norwegian
```
54%
av nordmenn bruker KI-verktøy

Norge er nummer 3 i verden for bruk av KI. Når noen i Bergen
spør ChatGPT «hva skjer denne helgen?», jobber vi for at Gåri
dukker opp som kilde — med kuraterte sider optimalisert for
det folk faktisk spør om.

Kilder: SSB 2025 · Microsoft AI Diffusion Report 2025 · Arbeidsliv i Norden · ChatGPT Statistics
```

#### English
```
54%
of Norwegians use AI tools

Norway is number 3 in the world for AI usage. When someone
in Bergen asks ChatGPT "what's on this weekend?", we work to
make Gåri appear as a source — with curated pages optimized
for what people actually ask about.

Sources: Statistics Norway 2025 · Microsoft AI Diffusion Report 2025 · Nordic Labour Journal · ChatGPT Statistics
```

**Why this works:**
- "54%" as a giant red number — stops scrolling, creates curiosity. Backed by SSB data.
- "Norge er nummer 3 i verden" — pride factor, makes AI feel relevant and local.
- "jobber vi for at Gåri dukker opp som kilde" — honest. We optimize, we don't guarantee.
- "kuraterte sider optimalisert for det folk faktisk spør om" — explains the mechanism without jargon.
- Source citations with links — builds trust with data-driven venue owners.

**What changed from v1:**
- Replaced "Visste du dette?" with hard SSB data (54% stat)
- Replaced "Gåri er en av kildene som blir sitert" with honest "jobber vi for at Gåri dukker opp"
- Removed "med lenke rett til arrangementet" — we make curated pages, not direct event links
- Added source references with links

### SECTION 3: Zero setup

**Strategy:** Show them they don't need to do anything. Automatic. Plus a venue lookup tool so they can check if they're already on Gåri.

#### Norwegian
```
Du trenger ikke gjøre noe

Gåri er et system som finner arrangementene dine der du
allerede legger dem ut — på nettsiden din, i billettplattformen,
eller i kalenderen. Du trenger ikke endre arbeidsvanene dine.
Alt skjer automatisk, to ganger daglig.

[Sjekk om du allerede er på Gåri: _______________]

→ Match: "✓ Grieghallen er allerede på Gåri!"
→ No match: "Fant ikke et treff — men det er helt gratis å bli
   lagt til. Vi ønsker å promotere det rike kulturlivet i Bergen."
   + inline form: [nettside-URL] [e-post] [Sjekk nettsiden min]
```

#### English
```
You don't need to do anything

Gåri is a system that finds your events where you already
publish them — on your website, in your ticketing platform,
or in your calendar. You don't need to change your workflow.
Everything happens automatically, twice daily.

[Check if you're already on Gåri: _______________]
```

**Why this works:**
- "Du trenger ikke gjøre noe" — the strongest possible reassurance. No work, no setup.
- "Gåri er et system som finner" — product as subject, not a person.
- Venue lookup: interactive, builds engagement. If they find themselves → trust. If not → free signup form right there.
- The "not found" path says it's FREE and leads to an inline form (nettside + e-post), not the paid CTA section.

**What changed from v1:**
- Replaced "43 kilder" social proof with zero-setup messaging
- Added interactive venue lookup with ~40 known venues
- "Not found" state has inline form for free onboarding, not a redirect to paid CTA
- Removed venue name pills from this section

### SECTION 4: How it works (3 steps)

**Strategy:** Three steps, each under 25 words. Lead with the outcome, not the process.

#### Norwegian

**1. Gåri samler alt**
Konserter, teater, mat, festivaler, familieaktiviteter, turer — 43 kilder i Bergen, oppdatert kl. 06 og 18 hver dag.

**2. Folk finner deg**
Gjennom AI-søk, Google, 13 kuraterte sider på gaari.no og ukentlige nyhetsbrev. Mange oppdager ting de aldri ville søkt etter.

**3. Klikket går til deg**
Hvert arrangement linker til din billettside. Gåri selger aldri billetter — vi sender publikum videre til deg.

#### English

**1. Gåri collects everything**
Concerts, theatre, food, festivals, family activities, tours — 43 sources in Bergen, updated at 06:00 and 18:00 every day.

**2. People find you**
Through AI search, Google, 13 curated pages on gaari.no and weekly newsletters. Many discover things they'd never have searched for.

**3. The click goes to you**
Every event links to your ticket page. Gåri never sells tickets — we send audiences your way.

**Why this works:**
- "Gåri samler alt" — uses product name as subject. Clear who does the work.
- "Folk finner deg" — the subject is "folk" and the object is "deg" (you). Powerful sentence.
- "Klikket går til deg" — specific (it's a click), direct (it goes to you).
- "Gåri selger aldri billetter" — states the rule clearly. Sounds like a promise, not a policy.
- "Oppdatert kl. 06 og 18 hver dag" — more specific than "to ganger daglig." Specific times feel more real.

**What I cut:**
- "Bergensere, studenter, turister og familier finner arrangementer gjennom..." — don't list the audience, it's padding
- "sosiale medier" from the channel list — it's paused, don't mention it

### SECTION 5: What you get (4 feature blocks)

**Strategy:** Each block starts with the most concrete, surprising thing. Not the category name, not the mechanism — the thing that makes them go "huh."

#### Norwegian

**Arrangementene dine i AI-svar**
Når folk spør ChatGPT om Bergen, dukker du opp. Gåri er en av kildene som blir sitert — en kanal de fleste arrangører ikke vet om ennå.

**Først på kuraterte sider**
Arrangementene dine vises øverst på sider som «Denne helgen» og «Konserter denne uken». 13 sider, tusenvis av besøk. Alltid merket som fremhevet.

**I nyhetsbrevet hver uke**
Arrangementene dine rett i innboksen til bergensere som planlegger helgen. Sendes hver torsdag.

**Tall på hva det ga deg**
Månedlig rapport med klikk fra Gåri til nettsiden din, hvilke arrangementer som traff best, og AI-synlighetsdata.

#### English

**Your events in AI answers**
When people ask ChatGPT about Bergen, you show up. Gåri is one of the sources that gets cited — a channel most organizers don't know about yet.

**First on curated pages**
Your events appear at the top of pages like "This Weekend" and "Concerts This Week". 13 pages, thousands of visits. Always labeled as featured.

**In the newsletter every week**
Your events straight in the inbox of people in Bergen planning their weekend. Sent every Thursday.

**Numbers on what it did for you**
Monthly report with clicks from Gåri to your website, which events performed best, and AI visibility data.

**Why this works:**
- "Arrangementene dine i AI-svar" — not "Synlig i AI-søk" (the old heading). The old heading is about a feature. The new one is about THEIR events.
- "Når folk spør ChatGPT om Bergen, dukker du opp" — "du" (you), not "dine arrangementer." Makes it personal.
- "Først på kuraterte sider" — not "Fremhevet på kuraterte sider." "Først" (first) is what they actually get. "Fremhevet" is the mechanism.
- "Tall på hva det ga deg" — not "Månedlig rapport." "Tall" (numbers) is more tangible. "Hva det ga deg" (what it gave you) focuses on value.
- "Sendes hver torsdag" — a concrete detail. When something has a schedule, it feels real.

**What I cut:**
- All blocks were 25+ words saying one thing. Now each leads with the punch, follows with one supporting detail.

### SECTION 6: Bytorget (philosophy)

**Strategy:** Three sentences. Get to the punchline fast.

#### Norwegian
```
Bergens digitale bytorg

Den store konserthallen trekker folk til torget. Den lille
bokklubben blir oppdaget av noen som bare kom for konserten.
Det er ikke veldedighet — det er god forretning for alle.
```

#### English
```
Bergen's digital town square

The big concert hall draws people to the square. The small
book club gets discovered by someone who just came for the concert.
It's not charity — it's good business for everyone.
```

**Why this works:**
- Three sentences. The old version was three paragraphs.
- I cut the "Gåri er bygget på samme idé" middle paragraph entirely. It was explaining the metaphor you just made. Trust the reader.
- The punchline — "Det er ikke veldedighet. Det er god forretning for alle." — now lands immediately after the setup, not after 80 words of elaboration.

**What I cut:**
- "Et bytorg fungerer fordi alle er der." — Obvious. The examples make this point better.
- "Når alle arrangementene i Bergen er samlet på ett sted, vinner alle — store og små." — Restates what the two examples already said.

### SECTION 7: Transparency

**Strategy:** Two sentences. Confidence, not disclaimer.

#### Norwegian
```
Ingen skjulte triks

Fremhevede arrangementer er alltid tydelig merket.
Du får alltid data på hva plasseringen ga deg.
```

#### English
```
No hidden tricks

Featured events are always clearly labeled.
You always get data on what the placement delivered.
```

**Why this works:**
- "Ingen skjulte triks" — not "Transparent alltid" (sounds like a legal page).
- Two sentences. The old version had four sentences explaining that organic content isn't pushed aside. That's important, but it reads like Terms of Service. If a venue owner cares about that detail, they'll ask in conversation.
- "Du får alltid data" — keeps the value promise (you get something, not just a label).

**What I cut:**
- "Vi flytter aldri organisk innhold for å gjøre plass — fremhevet synlighet er en tilleggsposisjon, ikke en erstatning." — True and important, but reads like legalese on a marketing page. Save this for the conversation.

## Copy rules that apply to everything above

1. **"Gåri" as product subject, "vi" for actions.** "Gåri samler alt som skjer" and "Vi sender publikum videre til deg." Never "jeg" or "meg". Never "vår plattform."
2. **Start sentences with the venue's thing, not Gåri's thing.** "Arrangementene dine dukker opp" not "Gåri gjør arrangementene dine synlige."
3. **No word appears that a venue owner wouldn't use at lunch.** If they wouldn't say "fremhevet synlighet" in conversation, neither should we.
4. **Every paragraph under 35 words.** If it's longer, it has two ideas — split it.
5. **Headings under 6 words.** "Visste du dette?" (3). "43 kilder." (2). "3 måneder gratis" (3).
6. **Concrete numbers beat vague claims.** "13 sider" not "flere kuraterte sider." "Kl. 06 og 18" not "to ganger daglig."
7. **The English isn't translated — it's rewritten.** Each version sounds natural in its own language.
